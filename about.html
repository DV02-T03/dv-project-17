About the Project

Understanding Australia's road safety enforcement through data visualisation

Project Background

This project visualises road safety enforcement data from the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE), a national record of traffic law enforcement in Australia.

The dataset tracks the volume and methods of fines issued for offences like speeding, mobile phone use, and drug driving across states and territories over time.

Data Sources

The visualisations are based on official BITRE data spanning from 2010 to 2024, including:

  • Speeding infringement statistics
  • Detection method breakdowns
  • Jurisdictional comparisons
  • Temporal trends analysis

BITRE Data Analysis

Why Road Safety Enforcement Matters

Road safety enforcement saves lives by deterring dangerous behaviours like speeding, which directly prevents crashes, injuries, and fatalities on our roads. Effective enforcement strategies contribute to:

Reduced Fatalities

Lower road trauma and preventable deaths

Economic Savings

Reduced healthcare and accident costs

Safer Communities

Improved safety for all road users

Our Target Audience

Primary Audience

Policymakers and transport planners in state transport departments who need to compare enforcement effectiveness across jurisdictions to reshape road safety strategies.

Transport Ministers Road Safety Directors Policy Advisors
Secondary Audience

Transport analysts who perform in-depth analysis to identify trends and produce reports, and the general public/media who help spread awareness about speeding enforcement importance.
